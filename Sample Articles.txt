After Diwali celebrations, Delhi and its surrounding National Capital Region (NCR) have been struggling with severely deteriorated air quality. 
The combination of firecrackers from the festivities and extensive crop residue burning in Punjab and Haryana has intensified pollution levels. 
With over 3,000 stubble-burning incidents recorded, particularly from farmers who find it challenging to implement sustainable disposal due to high costs, pollution levels have spiked. 
The Air Quality Index (AQI) has reached hazardous levels in many parts of the capital, with particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) concentrations surpassing safe limits. 
Health advisories warn residents, especially those with respiratory conditions, to minimize outdoor activities and utilize masks and air purifiers indoors.

Indian Army Resumes Patrols in Ladakh amid China Tensions
Amid renewed tensions with China, India has resumed strategic patrols in the contested regions of Demchok and Depsang in Eastern Ladakh. 
These areas have been focal points of the India-China border dispute along the Line of Actual Control (LAC),where both countries have engaged in multiple face-offs and skirmishes over the past few years. 
The decision to resume patrols is seen as part of India’s broader effort to assert territorial claims and ensure security in response to Chinese infrastructure development and increased troop presence along the border.
India has also invested in bolstering infrastructure along the LAC, including roads and high-altitude military installations, to improve the supply chain and support logistics for deployed forces.
